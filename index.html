About

Hello, my name is Raúl Jiménez Carrillo also know Dio Joestar as nickname. I have 24 years old and I'm from Barcelona, Spain.


Since I was a child, I always enjoyed modding and personalizing my computer and video games, from changing the sounds until making new maps.


Right now I'm working as technician for Venture in the HP offices at Sant Cugat del Vallès. I work there testing new printers that are large format, reporting errors, cleaning the printheads, checking the quality of the prints...

RETF2

In July 2014, I was a member of the crew Bad Wolf team who organized the Retrospective event at the Euskal Encounter 22 LAN party in Bilbao, Spain.


With two other friends, we designed, modeled, textured, and rigged two medals for the Team Fortress 2 video game, one as a winner prize and other for the participants. Both of them were added to the game by VALVE.

At 2015 we did it again, this time with support from eXtelevision. The Retrospective 2 tournament had over 2300 participants in a total of 5 formats (Highlander, 6v6, RSP, Arena respawn, and Ultiduo).

OnlineSDK

Name shortened from Spanish Development Kit, it was a spanish project, made from scratch, focused on teaching or expand their knowledge in the creation and modification on content related to the game engine of Half-Life 2, the Source engine.


It was born as a site where I could share my documentation about modding and a way for learn HTML, CSS, and PHP on my way.


Right now it's only an abandoned project.

SteamCMD GUI

C:\Users\DioJoestar>type steamcmd-gui_desc.txt

Time ago I used to use batch scripts for install and run game servers. Tired of navigating through a poor menu using the numbers, I made the next step, build a graphical interface. For that task I chose Visual Basic for start learning a new programing language.


Step by step, I build SteamCMD GUI. As the name suggests, is a graphical interface for the Steam Console Client (SteamCMD), a command-line version of Steam for set up dedicated servers.


A lot of people contributed with this software making tutorials in different languages, helping with the code, providing feedback and ideas... even was recommended in PCGamer.
